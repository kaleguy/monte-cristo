# Key Characters in The Count of Monte Cristo

## The Protagonist

### Edmond Dantès / The Count of Monte Cristo
- **Former identity**: Young sailor, first mate of the Pharaon
- **Transformed identity**: The Count of Monte Cristo, wealthy aristocrat
- **Other aliases**: Lord Wilmore, Abbé Busoni, Sinbad the Sailor
- **Age at imprisonment**: 19 years old
- **Age at escape**: 33 years old
- **Age during revenge**: 43-49 years old

## The Enemies

### Fernand Mondego (Count de Morcerf)
- **Crime**: Jealousy and betrayal - delivered the accusation letter
- **Relationship**: Rival for Mercédès' love
- **Later position**: General, Count, French peer
- **Family**: Married Mercédès, son Albert
- **Fate**: Suicide after public disgrace

### Baron Danglars
- **Crime**: Wrote the treacherous letter out of professional jealousy
- **Relationship**: Supercargo on the Pharaon
- **Later position**: Wealthy banker, Baron
- **Family**: Wife Hermione, daughter Eugénie
- **Fate**: Financial ruin and poverty

### Gérard de Villefort
- **Crime**: Condemned Dantès to prison to hide evidence
- **Relationship**: Royal prosecutor
- **Later position**: Royal Prosecutor in Paris
- **Family**: Two wives (Renée, then Héloïse), children Valentine and Édouard
- **Fate**: Insanity after family destruction

## The Innocent Victims

### Mercédès
- **Role**: Dantès' fiancée, later Fernand's wife
- **Character**: Loyal but ultimately accepts Fernand
- **Fate**: Recognizes the Count, loses everything, lives in poverty

### Albert de Morcerf
- **Role**: Son of Fernand and Mercédès
- **Character**: Honorable young man
- **Fate**: Discovers truth, challenges then reconciles with the Count

### Valentine de Villefort
- **Role**: Villefort's daughter from first marriage
- **Character**: Pure and innocent
- **Love interest**: Maximilian Morrel
- **Fate**: Targeted for murder, saved by the Count

## The Allies

### Abbé Faria
- **Role**: Dantès' mentor in prison
- **Contribution**: Educated Dantès, revealed the treasure location
- **Fate**: Dies in prison, allows Dantès' escape

### Haydée
- **Role**: Daughter of Ali Pasha, the Count's ward
- **Purpose**: Key witness against Fernand
- **Relationship**: Falls in love with the Count

### Luigi Vampa
- **Role**: Italian bandit chief
- **Relationship**: Ally of the Count
- **Service**: Helps with the revenge against Danglars

### Monsieur Morrel
- **Role**: Dantès' former employer, ship owner
- **Character**: Honorable, nearly ruined financially
- **Relationship**: The Count saves him and his family

### Maximilian Morrel
- **Role**: Son of Monsieur Morrel
- **Character**: Young army officer, deeply honorable
- **Love interest**: Valentine de Villefort
- **Fate**: Despairs when Valentine "dies," contemplates suicide

### Bertuccio
- **Role**: The Count's loyal steward
- **Background**: Former smuggler with connection to Villefort
- **Service**: Manages the Count's affairs, provides information

### Ali
- **Role**: The Count's mute Nubian servant
- **Character**: Completely loyal, deadly when needed

## Other Important Characters

### Caderousse
- **Role**: Neighbor who knew of the conspiracy but did nothing
- **Character**: Cowardly and greedy
- **Fate**: The Count gives him opportunities; dies trying to rob the Count

### Benedetto / Andrea Cavalcanti
- **True identity**: Villefort's illegitimate son
- **Role**: Used by the Count to expose Villefort
- **Character**: Criminal and opportunist
- **Fate**: Arrested, exposes Villefort in court

### Eugénie Danglars
- **Role**: Danglars' daughter
- **Character**: Independent, artistic, rejects traditional marriage
- **Fate**: Escapes arranged marriage, pursues her own life
