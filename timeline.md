# Timeline of The Count of Monte Cristo

## Early Life and Betrayal (1815)

### February 1815
- **Edmond Dantès** returns to Marseilles as captain of the Pharaon
- He is 19 years old
- Engaged to marry **Mercédès**
- Promised promotion by ship-owner **Monsieur Morrel**

### February 24, 1815
- Napoleon escapes from Elba (historical event)

### February 28, 1815
- Dantès' engagement feast
- **Danglars** and **Fernand** write the accusation letter
- **Caderousse** watches but does nothing
- Dantès is arrested

### March 1815
- Dantès interrogated by deputy prosecutor **Villefort**
- Villefort discovers letter was for his father
- Burns evidence, sends Dantès to Château d'If

## The Prison Years (1815-1829)

### 1815-1821
- Dantès imprisoned in isolation
- Becomes suicidal
- Hears scratching sounds

### 1821
- Meets **Abbé Faria** through tunnel
- Begins education: languages, sciences, history, philosophy

### 1829 (14 years after imprisonment)
- Abbé Faria dies
- Dantès escapes in the burial sack
- February 28, 1829 - Dantès arrives at Monte Cristo island
- Discovers the treasure

## Transformation Period (1829-1838)

### 1829-1838
- Dantès travels the world
- Learns about his enemies' fates
- Becomes the Count of Monte Cristo
- Establishes multiple identities
- Accumulates more wealth
- Plans his revenge
- Rescues Haydée

## The Return (1838)

### May 1838
- The Count arrives in Paris
- He is now about 43 years old
- Begins implementing his plans
- Establishes residence in Paris
- Makes contact with Parisian society

## The Revenge (1838-1844)

### 1838-1839
- Cultivates relationships with targets
- Saves Morrel family from ruin
- Positions himself in society
- Manipulates financial markets
- Introduces Haydée to society

### 1840-1842
- Deepens involvement with target families
- Andrea Cavalcanti introduced to society
- Financial schemes against Danglars intensify
- Poisoning plot in Villefort household begins
- Valentine targeted

### 1843
- Telegraph fraud costs Danglars 1 million francs
- Spanish bond default - Danglars loses 3 million francs
- Haydée testifies against Morcerf
- Morcerf stripped of titles
- Albert de Morcerf challenges the Count to duel
- Mercédès pleads with the Count
- Morcerf commits suicide

### 1844 (Final Year)
- Danglars loses more money through various schemes
- Andrea Cavalcanti engaged to Eugénie Danglars
- Andrea arrested at wedding
- Benedetto reveals he is Villefort's son
- Villefort publicly exposed
- Héloïse poisons herself and Édouard
- Villefort goes insane
- Danglars flees with 5 million francs
- Danglars captured by Vampa
- Danglars' fortune consumed
- Count reveals identity to Danglars
- Valentine "dies" but is actually drugged
- Maximilian despairs
- Valentine revealed to be alive
- Count questions his revenge

## Resolution (1844)

### October 1844
- Count saves Valentine and Maximilian
- Provides them with fortune
- Leaves France with Haydée
- Final letter: "Wait and Hope"

## Summary Statistics

- **Years imprisoned**: 14 years (1815-1829)
- **Years of preparation**: 9 years (1829-1838)
- **Years of revenge**: 6 years (1838-1844)
- **Total time from arrest to resolution**: 29 years
- **Dantès' age at arrest**: 19
- **Dantès' age at escape**: 33
- **Dantès' age at resolution**: 48
